We just had a catastrophic failure during an IRS compliance audit regarding our automated refund API. 

A customer bought 3 identical T-shirts for $30 total ($10 each). They called support to complain about a shipping delay, so we issued a flat $10 goodwill refund across the order. 

The system correctly calculated that each T-shirt should get a proportional refund of $3.33. However, when our accounting system tallied the ledger, the total refund disbursed was only $9.99 ($3.33 x 3). We literally lost a penny into the void. 

While 1 cent seems small, this happens thousands of times a day, and our books are now off by hundreds of dollars. 

The `verify_total_refund` audit function is failing. The prorated refund function MUST ensure that the sum of `refund_applied` across all items EXACTLY matches the requested `refund_amount`, down to the last cent. You need to handle the remainder properly, usually by applying any leftover pennies to the very last item in the list. Fix this immediately.
